# 20 Example User Personas  
*Designed for Research Through Design (RtD) contexts*

Each persona below is:
- Goal-oriented and behavior-centered  
- Explicit about constraints and tensions  
- Actionable for design decisions  
- Framed as a **hypothesis**, not a fictional character  

---

## 1. The Time‑Constrained Operator
**Core Goal:** Complete required tasks with minimal interruption  
**Behaviors:** Prioritizes speed, skips optional steps, relies on defaults  
**Constraints:** Limited time, frequent context switching  
**Tension:** Speed vs. correctness  
**Design Implication:** Interfaces must surface critical actions immediately and tolerate partial engagement  

---

## 2. The Risk‑Averse Validator
**Core Goal:** Avoid errors and unintended consequences  
**Behaviors:** Double‑checks inputs, seeks confirmation, prefers proven methods  
**Constraints:** High accountability, low tolerance for failure  
**Tension:** Thoroughness vs. efficiency  
**Design Implication:** Systems should provide traceability, previews, and rollback  

---

## 3. The Experimental Explorer
**Core Goal:** Discover new possibilities and approaches  
**Behaviors:** Tries unconventional paths, probes edge cases, iterates rapidly  
**Constraints:** Limited guardrails, incomplete information  
**Tension:** Freedom vs. reliability  
**Design Implication:** Designs should support safe experimentation and rapid iteration  

---

## 4. The Compliance‑Bound Practitioner
**Core Goal:** Meet formal standards and regulatory requirements  
**Behaviors:** Follows prescribed workflows, documents decisions meticulously  
**Constraints:** External rules, audits, legal oversight  
**Tension:** Innovation vs. compliance  
**Design Implication:** Tools must embed compliance without blocking work  

---

## 5. The Resource‑Limited Improviser
**Core Goal:** Achieve acceptable outcomes with minimal resources  
**Behaviors:** Repurposes tools, creates workarounds, prioritizes essentials  
**Constraints:** Budget, equipment, or staffing limits  
**Tension:** Quality vs. feasibility  
**Design Implication:** Designs should degrade gracefully and support low‑resource use  

---

## 6. The Outcome‑Driven Manager
**Core Goal:** Deliver results on schedule  
**Behaviors:** Focuses on milestones, delegates details, tracks progress metrics  
**Constraints:** Time pressure, coordination overhead  
**Tension:** Oversight vs. autonomy  
**Design Implication:** Systems should summarize status and surface risks early  

---

## 7. The Detail‑Oriented Analyst
**Core Goal:** Understand underlying mechanisms and causes  
**Behaviors:** Examines data deeply, questions assumptions, builds models  
**Constraints:** Cognitive load, complex information  
**Tension:** Depth vs. timeliness  
**Design Implication:** Designs should allow progressive disclosure of detail  

---

## 8. The Novice Learner
**Core Goal:** Build basic competence and confidence  
**Behaviors:** Follows examples, seeks guidance, hesitates to deviate  
**Constraints:** Limited domain knowledge  
**Tension:** Learning vs. productivity  
**Design Implication:** Interfaces should scaffold learning without overwhelming  

---

## 9. The Expert Specialist
**Core Goal:** Execute advanced tasks efficiently  
**Behaviors:** Uses shortcuts, customizes tools, bypasses defaults  
**Constraints:** Frustration with simplified systems  
**Tension:** Power vs. accessibility  
**Design Implication:** Designs should expose advanced controls without harming novices  

---

## 10. The Collaboration‑Focused Contributor
**Core Goal:** Coordinate effectively with others  
**Behaviors:** Shares intermediate work, seeks alignment, documents decisions  
**Constraints:** Communication overhead, version conflicts  
**Tension:** Individual efficiency vs. team clarity  
**Design Implication:** Systems should make collaboration visible and frictionless  

---

## 11. The Accountability‑Driven Reporter
**Core Goal:** Provide defensible explanations of work  
**Behaviors:** Records rationale, keeps logs, references sources  
**Constraints:** External scrutiny  
**Tension:** Documentation vs. progress  
**Design Implication:** Designs should capture rationale as a byproduct of action  

---

## 12. The Context‑Switcher
**Core Goal:** Maintain continuity across fragmented work sessions  
**Behaviors:** Leaves placeholders, relies on reminders, revisits prior states  
**Constraints:** Interruptions, multitasking  
**Tension:** Continuity vs. distraction  
**Design Implication:** Systems should support resumability and state recall  

---

## 13. The Optimization‑Focused Improver
**Core Goal:** Incrementally improve performance or efficiency  
**Behaviors:** Measures outcomes, compares variants, refines parameters  
**Constraints:** Diminishing returns  
**Tension:** Optimization vs. exploration  
**Design Implication:** Designs should support comparison and iteration history  

---

## 14. The Ethics‑Motivated Stakeholder
**Core Goal:** Ensure actions align with values and impacts  
**Behaviors:** Questions implications, raises concerns, seeks transparency  
**Constraints:** Limited authority to change systems  
**Tension:** Moral responsibility vs. practical constraints  
**Design Implication:** Designs should surface ethical consequences and tradeoffs  

---

## 15. The Tool‑Skeptical Traditionalist
**Core Goal:** Rely on trusted, familiar methods  
**Behaviors:** Resists new tools, prefers manual control  
**Constraints:** Past negative experiences with change  
**Tension:** Stability vs. innovation  
**Design Implication:** New designs must demonstrate clear, immediate value  

---

## 16. The Automation‑Seeking Optimizer
**Core Goal:** Reduce repetitive manual effort  
**Behaviors:** Scripts tasks, looks for batch operations, standardizes inputs  
**Constraints:** Setup cost, brittleness of automation  
**Tension:** Efficiency vs. flexibility  
**Design Implication:** Systems should make automation transparent and adjustable  

---

## 17. The Evidence‑Hungry Decision Maker
**Core Goal:** Make choices backed by credible data  
**Behaviors:** Requests metrics, compares sources, challenges claims  
**Constraints:** Data quality and availability  
**Tension:** Confidence vs. uncertainty  
**Design Implication:** Designs should communicate uncertainty and provenance clearly  

---

## 18. The Failure‑Avoidant Maintainer
**Core Goal:** Keep systems running without disruption  
**Behaviors:** Monitors stability, avoids risky changes, schedules maintenance  
**Constraints:** Legacy systems, technical debt  
**Tension:** Reliability vs. improvement  
**Design Implication:** Designs should isolate risk and support incremental change  

---

## 19. The Insight‑Seeking Reflector
**Core Goal:** Learn from past actions to improve future ones  
**Behaviors:** Reviews outcomes, annotates decisions, looks for patterns  
**Constraints:** Limited time for reflection  
**Tension:** Reflection vs. forward momentum  
**Design Implication:** Systems should enable lightweight retrospection  

---

## 20. The Boundary‑Pushing Innovator
**Core Goal:** Extend what is currently possible  
**Behaviors:** Challenges assumptions, combines tools in novel ways  
**Constraints:** Institutional resistance, uncertainty  
**Tension:** Vision vs. feasibility  
**Design Implication:** Designs should support extensibility and reinterpretation  

---

## Closing Note

These personas are **living hypotheses**.  
They should be tested, revised, merged, or discarded as design work generates new evidence.
